Mountain Rescue Team works for free for your leisure and safety

SHI MIN CHUA

At the beginning of 2026, an outpouring of donation has been made to Wasdale Mountain Rescue Team to help cover the cost of hotel fee after news of two walkers not paying the hotel that took them in after the team rescued them and arranged for them to spend the night in the hotel. However, the cost of the rescue is not limited to the hotel fee. According to the incident report, the rescue involved 8 team members who have spent their evening until after midnight (7.1 hours) to rescue the men. Of course the team did not get paid for their labour, which would be 8 x 7.1 = 57 hours in total, because mountain rescue teams in the UK are operated by volunteers. Besides this recent incident, back in February 2021, a headline reported a mountain rescuer fell for 150m and suffered spinal fractures while out in a mission around midnight to assist a camper with chest pain. This incident actually happened during Covid-19 lockdown when people were supposed not to mingle outside their bubbles. Despite these human cost and injuries, almost all the time, the mountain rescue teams have been continuing doing their good work, not to mention the ever-increasing incidents.

Disclaimer

  • The opinion expressed here is my own and does not represent any mountain rescue team. Despite me having read quite a lot of the incident reports and conducting the analyses presented here, My understandding on mountain rescue is highly likely different from the team members who have done all the hardwork.
  • As a leisure hiker who goes to Lake District every year, I did this piece to express my personal admiration to these unsung heroes who sacrificed their time and sometimes put themselves at risk to ensure all walkers could enjoy the beautiful scenery in Lake District safely. I am in awe with their selfless efforts, whether during summer when everybody is enjoying holiday except them, or during cold winter night when visibility is low on the mountains, or during rainy days when walking paths become flowing streams.
  • I hope this piece will raise awareness in future hikers such that when they set up for a walk, they would have the mountain rescue team in their mind and they do not risk their own safety that in turn requires the team risking theirs.

Methodology

  • I excluded Incident Type marked as 'Other' for simplicity. They are equally significant and taking time and effort from the team. These 'Other' events included assisting local emergency, saving dog in distress on mountains, rescuing people from lakes, clearing road during winter, assisting flood rescues.
